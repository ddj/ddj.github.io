Habito News

    Mobile News Apps

Project description

UCL Interaction Group asked for help to create an Android mobile news application that had adaptive user interfaces which can change to match a users reading habits and style. The project involved research, design and implementation of UI components centered around a users navigation of news categories and article reading styles.

Research

The research work behind Habito News involves monitoring user's news reading behaviour, recognising patterns of such behaviour in order to building user models, and finally developing an automated adaptive user interface that would personalise news reading experience on mobile devices.

I conducted analysis into news reading habits through online questionnaires, focus groups and contextual enquiry, and as a result put forward three news reader personas: Trackers, Reviewers and Dippers, I then designed three variant interfaces for each type, that were aimed to improve their navigation experience and behaviour and evaluated their use with 18 participants in a controlled laboratory study. This research work was published and can be viewed at Mobile HCI 2015, Copenhagen, Denmark
